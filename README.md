# HR Data Analytics Dashboard

An interactive **HR Data Analytics Dashboard** built in **Microsoft Excel** to analyze workforce distribution, employee attrition, salary trends, job satisfaction, and employee performance. This project helps HR teams monitor key workforce metrics and make data-driven decisions for improving employee retention and organizational performance.

---

# 📌 Project Overview

The objective of this project is to transform raw HR data into meaningful business insights through an interactive dashboard.

The dashboard enables HR managers to monitor employee demographics, workforce distribution, attrition trends, salary analysis, job satisfaction, and work-life balance using dynamic visualizations and KPI reporting.

---

# 🎯 Business Problem

Employee attrition is one of the biggest challenges faced by organizations.

The HR department requires an interactive dashboard to:

- Monitor employee attrition
- Analyze workforce distribution
- Identify high-risk departments and job roles
- Track employee satisfaction
- Support strategic HR decision-making

---

# 📊 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- 👨‍💼 Total Employees
- 🟢 Active Employees
- 🚪 Attrition Count
- 📉 Attrition Rate (%)
- 🎂 Average Employee Age
- 💰 Average Monthly Income
- 📅 Average Years at Company
- ⭐ Average Job Satisfaction
- 👨 Male Employees
- 👩 Female Employees

# 📈 Dashboard Features

The dashboard includes the following interactive visualizations:

- Employees by Department
- Gender Distribution
- Attrition Rate by Department
- Attrition Rate by Job Role
- Attrition Rate by Age Group
- Average Monthly Income by Department
- Average Job Satisfaction by Department
- Average Work-Life Balance by Department
- Average Years at Company by Department

---

# 📋 Business Questions

This dashboard answers important HR business questions such as:

- Which department has the highest workforce?
- Which department experiences the highest attrition?
- Which job role has the highest employee turnover?
- Which age group is most likely to leave?
- What is the gender distribution across the organization?
- Which department offers the highest average salary?
- Which department has the highest employee satisfaction?
- How many years do employees stay with the company on average?

---

# 💡 Key Insights

- Research & Development has the largest workforce.
- Sales department records the highest attrition rate.
- Employees under 25 years show the highest attrition.
- Sales Representatives experience the highest employee turnover.
- HR department reports the highest work-life balance.
- Average employee job satisfaction is moderate across departments.

---

# 🚀 Business Recommendations

- Improve retention strategies for the Sales department.
- Strengthen onboarding and mentoring programs for younger employees.
- Review compensation and career growth opportunities for high-risk job roles.
- Enhance employee engagement initiatives.
- Improve work-life balance through employee wellness programs.
